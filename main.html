By January 2013, Attenborough had collected 32 honorary degrees from British universities,[79] more than any other person.[80][81] In 1980, he was honoured by the Open University with whom he has had a close association throughout his career. He also has honorary Doctor of Science degrees from Durham University (1982)[82] and the University of Cambridge (1984)[83] and an honorary Doctor of Philosophy degree from the University of Oxford (1988).[83] In 2006, the two eldest Attenborough brothers returned to their home city to receive the title of Distinguished Honorary Fellows of the University of Leicester, "in recognition of a record of continuing distinguished service to the University."[84] David Attenborough was previously awarded an honorary Doctor of Letters degree by the university in 1970, and was made an honorary Freeman of the City of Leicester in 1990. In 2013, he was made an Honorary Freeman of the City of Bristol.[85] In 2010, he was awarded an Honorary Doctorate from Nelson Mandela Metropolitan University and Nottingham Trent University.[86] Attenborough has received the title Honorary Fellow from Clare College, Cambridge (1980), the Zoological Society of London (1998), the Linnean Society (1999), the Institute of Biology (Now the Royal Society of Biology) (2000) and the Society of Antiquaries (2007).
